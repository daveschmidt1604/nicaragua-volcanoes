Telica is a stratovolcano in northwestern Nicaragua, part of the Maribios volcanic chain. It reaches an elevation of 1,036 m and is among the most frequently erupting volcanoes in the country. The summit contains multiple nested craters with persistent fumarolic activity and a crater lake that periodically drains before eruptive episodes. Telica's eruptions are predominantly phreatic and Vulcanian in character, generating ash columns and ballistic ejecta that affect the surrounding agricultural lowlands and communities including the city of León. The volcano is continuously monitored by the Nicaraguan Institute of Territorial Studies (INETER).

Recent Activity

Telica has a documented eruption record dating back to 1527 and has erupted repeatedly throughout the historical period. The following records highlight notable eruptions and activity episodes from recent decades. For the complete eruption history, see the GVP entry ↗.

  • 1982 – February: several strong explosions generate ash columns reaching ~3.5 km altitude; ashfall affects nearby towns
  • 1987 – November: explosive eruption with ash emissions recorded
  • 1994 – July 31: gas-and-ash eruption column reaches ~800 m above the summit; phreatic activity with minor ash explosions continues until mid-August
  • 1999–2000 – August 1999: explosive eruption with ashfall reaching Chichigalpa (20 km WSW); lava lake observed in summit crater on 18 August; ash eruptions continue through February 2000, with intermittent activity and crater incandescence through 2002
  • 2007 – January–February: small ash eruptions producing plumes to ~1.5 km altitude
  • 2011 – March–June: VEI 2 eruption; most explosive episode since 1999; phreatic/amagmatic eruption driven by sealing of the hydrothermal system; ash columns reaching 1.5–2 km above the crater rim; activity intensifies from May 13 with daily explosions
  • 2015 – May 7: increased explosive activity with ash emissions and low-intensity earthquakes; ashfall affects surrounding communities and farmland
  • 2018 – June 21: phreatic explosion produces ash plume rising ~500 m above the crater; drifts east and southwest
  • 2021–2022 – April 2021: eruption begins with weak ash explosions and ash plumes; activity continues through March 2022
