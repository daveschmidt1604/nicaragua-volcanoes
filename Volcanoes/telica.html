Telica is a stratovolcano in northwestern Nicaragua, part of the Maribios volcanic chain. It reaches an elevation of 1,036 m and is among the most frequently erupting volcanoes in the country. The summit contains multiple nested craters with persistent fumarolic activity and a crater lake that periodically drains before eruptive episodes. Telica's eruptions are predominantly phreatic and Vulcanian in character, generating ash columns and ballistic ejecta that affect the surrounding agricultural lowlands and communities including the city of León. The volcano is continuously monitored by the Nicaraguan Institute of Territorial Studies (INETER).

Activity Timeline

  • 1994 – Phreatic explosions with ash ejections; minor ashfall reported in nearby communities
  • 1999 – Significant explosive eruptions producing ash plumes up to several kilometers height; ashfall on León
  • 2000 – Continued explosive episodes; seismic activity elevated throughout the year
  • 2010 – Series of Vulcanian explosions; ash clouds dispersed to the southwest
  • 2011 – Strongest activity in over a decade; multiple large explosions, ballistic ejecta, ashfall on farmland
  • 2012 – Moderate explosive episodes; crater lake partially drained prior to eruptions
  • 2013 – Renewed explosive activity; INETER raises alert level
  • 2015 – Multiple eruptive episodes throughout the year; ashfall affects crops and forces temporary evacuations
  • 2016 – Continued low-level explosions; elevated SO₂ flux and fumarolic activity
  • 2018 – Phreatic explosions with ash and steam; gas monitoring detects precursory SO₂ increase
  • 2022 – Renewed explosive activity; ash plumes and increased seismicity reported
