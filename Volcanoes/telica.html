Telica is a stratovolcano in northwestern Nicaragua, part of the Maribios volcanic chain. It reaches an elevation of 1,036 m and is among the most frequently erupting volcanoes in the country. The summit contains multiple nested craters with persistent fumarolic activity and a crater lake that periodically drains before eruptive episodes. Telica's eruptions are predominantly phreatic and Vulcanian in character, generating ash columns and ballistic ejecta that affect the surrounding agricultural lowlands and communities including the city of León. The volcano is continuously monitored by the Nicaraguan Institute of Territorial Studies (INETER).

Recent Activity

Telica has a documented eruption record dating back to 1527 and has erupted repeatedly throughout the historical period. The following records highlight notable eruptions and activity episodes from recent decades. For the complete eruption history, see the GVP entry ↗.

  • 1981 November – 1982 March: eruption following months of elevated seismicity; major explosions on 12 February (largest since 1976; blocks 2–3 m diameter ejected 200 m from crater rim; ash column to ~3.7–4.3 km asl) and 19 February (column to ~3.5 km asl); activity ends 2 March
  • 1987 November: Strombolian activity with incandescent column; fresh basaltic bombs ejected 50 m from the eastern crater rim; new vent formed in the crater; not directly observed by geologists — confirmed from resident reports and newspaper photographs
  • 1994 July: gas-and-ash eruption column reaches ~800 m above the summit on 31 July; phreatic activity with minor ash explosions continues until mid-August
  • 1999 August – 2000 February: explosive eruption with ashfall reaching Chichigalpa (20 km WSW); lava lake observed in summit crater on 18 August; ash eruptions continue through February 2000
  • 2001 January – 2001 December: prolonged low-level activity; ash plume ~200 m high on 17 January; minor ashfall within 500 m of crater; intermittent small explosions and incandescence observed throughout the year
  • 2004 March: small gas-and-ash explosion on 31 March; terminating event of the February–March 2004 eruptive phase
  • 2004 November – 2005 January: multiple ash explosions with significant gas and ash output
  • 2006 August: ash and gas explosions from a new vent on 4 and 6 August; preceded by elevated seismicity with 3,000–6,000 micro-earthquakes per month throughout January–November 2006
  • 2006 December – 2007 February: seismic character changes 25 November 2006; small explosions detected on 11 and 27 December; 9–10 January: phreatic explosions with gas-and-ash plume to ~1.5 km altitude drifting west; continued small explosions on 6, 15, and 17 February with ash plumes drifting southwest
  • 2007 October – 2008 February: new eruptive episode beginning late October; small gas-and-ash explosions recorded through February 2008; seismicity averaging 90–150 micro-earthquakes per day
  • 2008 July: small gas-and-ash explosions in early July
  • 2011 March – 2011 June: VEI 2 eruption; most explosive episode since 1999; phreatic/amagmatic eruption driven by sealing of the hydrothermal system; ash columns reaching 1.5–2 km above the crater rim; activity intensifies from 13 May with daily explosions
  • 2013 September: 4 small explosions on 25 September; one generated a plume 50 m above the crater rim; recorded by INETER
  • 2015 May: VEI 2 eruptive episode beginning 7 May; eruptive throughout the month with 902 seismically-detected explosions, 104 accompanied by volcanic ash; towns within 40 km in a generally W direction affected by ashfall; explosion on 12 May ejected rocks 400 m high to the W
  • 2015 September – 2016 May: VEI 2 eruption; explosive activity from 23 September with ashfall in surrounding communities; continued explosive activity through May 2016
  • 2018 June – 2018 August: phreatic eruption on 21 June; ash plume rising ~500 m above the crater, drifting east, south, and southwest; ejected tephra deposited within 1 km radius of the volcano; 15 August explosion generated an ash plume to 200 m above the crater rim
  • 2020 July – 2020 December: gas-and-ash eruption beginning 7 July with ashfall; renewed explosive activity from 30 November; a total of over 1,300 small explosions recorded from 30 November through early December
  • 2021 April – 2022 March: eruption begins with weak ash explosions and ash plumes; SO₂ emissions elevated throughout, peaking in late 2021; February 2022: ash emissions to ~1.5 km altitude drifting up to 55 km W, WSW, and SW; intermittent ash plumes on 1, 24, and 29 March rising to 1.2–1.5 km and drifting SW and W; last reported activity 29 March
  • 2025 January – 2025 February: multiple gas-and-ash emissions recorded by INETER; 13–14 January: 25 gas-and-ash explosions with ballistics ejected onto the flanks; 21–28 February: ash plumes rising to ~1.5 km altitude drifting up to 55 km WSW and SW
  • 2025 July – 2025 August: multiple ash-emission episodes; 18–20 July ash plumes drift ~13 km SW and W at ~1.5 km altitude; 31 July gas-and-ash column rises ~800 m above the summit with ashfall SW of the volcano; 7–8 August ash plumes drift up to 37 km WSW and SW at ~1.5 km altitude; phreatic activity with minor ash explosions continues to 12 August
