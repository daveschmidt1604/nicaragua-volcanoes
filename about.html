About the Project

This website presents field documentation and analysis of volcanic activity at three of Nicaragua's most persistently active volcanoes: Masaya, Telica, and Cerro Negro. The site was developed as part of a master's research project in Applied Geosciences at the University of Bremen, drawing on field imagery, drone footage, and eruption records compiled over multiple field campaigns in Nicaragua between 1994 and 2025.

The three volcanoes together represent the diversity of eruptive styles found along the Maribios volcanic chain — from the persistent lava lake and high-volume SO₂ degassing at Masaya, to the explosive Vulcanian activity at Telica, to the basaltic cinder cone dynamics of Cerro Negro, the youngest volcano in Central America.
